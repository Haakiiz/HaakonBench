# The Lazy Gladiator’s Guide to Aquatic Wealth
### A Level 60 Human Warrior's Guide to Stationary, Safe, and Highly Profitable Fishing in WoW Classic

Welcome, Champion of the Alliance. You’ve braved the depths of Blackwing Lair, survived the horrors of Ahn'Qiraj, and paid more gold in plate repair bills than most mages will ever see in their lifetimes. You are tired of chasing rage bars, tired of spamming Hamstring on slippery mages in Silithus, and tired of losing world buffs to a stealthy rogue. 

It is time to put down your *Ashkandi* (or your *Thunderfury*), pick up a fishing rod, and retire to the quiet waters of Azeroth. With **300 Fishing and 300 Cooking**, you are sitting on a goldmine. 

This guide is designed for **maximum gold per hour with zero movement and absolute safety**. No PvP, no PvE deaths, and no running around chasing pools. Just stand, cast, cook, and print gold.

## Chapter I: The Warrior's Tackle Box (Gear & Preparation)

Before you cast your first line, we need to ensure your fishing skill is artificially boosted. Even at 300 base skill, high-level zones require higher fishing stats to prevent the dreaded *"Your fish got away"* message.

### 1. The Weapon Swap
*   **The Pole:** Your primary weapon is no longer a sword. 
    *   *Budget Option:* **Big Iron Fishing Pole** (+20 Fishing). Found in Shellfish Traps in Desolace.
    *   *BiS Option:* **Arcanite Fishing Pole** (+35 Fishing). Won from the Stranglethorn Fishing Extravaganza.
*   **The Line:** Apply a **High-Test Eternium Line** (+5 Fishing) to your pole.
*   **The Boots:** **Lucky Fishing Boots** (+5 Fishing), a reward from the STV Fishing tournament.
*   **The Hat:** **Lucky Fishing Hat** (+5 Fishing).

### 2. The Consumables (Lures)
Never fish in high-level zones without a lure. 
*   **Aquadynamic Fish Lens** (+100 Fishing for 10 mins). Buy these in bulk on the Auction House or have an Engineer friend craft them.
*   **Bright Baubles** (+75 Fishing for 10 mins). Sold by any standard fishing vendor.

### 3. The Warrior Advantage
*   **Plate Armor:** Keep your plate armor on! If an incredibly lost Horde player or a rogue mob somehow wanders near you, your high armor and health pool give you plenty of time to react, swap back to your weapons, and execute them.
*   **Weapon Swap Macro:** 
    ```text
    /equip [noequipped:Fishing Poles] Your Fishing Pole Name
    /equip [equipped:Fishing Poles] Your Two-Handed Weapon Name
    ```

*   **Safety Rating:** 100% (Alliance Territory)
*   **Location:** Stand right on the wooden docks of Feathermoon Stronghold on Sardor Isle.
*   **Why it works:** You are surrounded by Level 55+ Elite Alliance Sentinels. If any Horde player steps foot on this dock, they are instantly turned into Swiss cheese. There are zero hostile PvE mobs. You can literally watch a movie on your second monitor.

    *   **Stonescale Eel** (Nighttime: 6 PM to 6 AM). High value for Alchemists (Stonescale Oil).
    *   **Winter Squid** (Seasonal: Sept 23 – March 19).

    *   **Raw Spotted Yellowtail** (Daytime: 6 AM to 6 PM).

### Spot 2: Lake Elune’ara (Moonglade)
*   **Safety Rating:** 100% (Neutral Sanctuary)
*   **Location:** Stand on the wooden docks or the sandy shores of Lake Elune'ara, just south of Nighthaven.
*   **Why it works:** Moonglade is a Cenarion Sanctuary. PvP is strictly prohibited by law. If any player attacks you, the Level 60 Elite Moonglade Wardens will instantly teleport to them and obliterate them. It is arguably the most peaceful place in the game.
*   **What you catch:**
    *   **Raw Nightfin Snapper** (Nighttime: 6 PM to 6 AM).

To make this 100% safe from PvE, you must walk along the eastern mountain wall and stand on one of the steep rock ledges overlooking the water. The local hippogryphs and green dragons cannot pathfind to you, and Horde players almost never visit this remote corner.
*   **What you catch:**
    *   **Raw Nightfin Snapper** (6 PM to 6 AM). Excellent catch rates here.

## Chapter III: The Culinary Alchemy (Cooking for Profit)

Raw fish sell okay, but **cooked fish sell for a premium**. Raiders do not want to buy raw fish and cook it themselves—they buy their raid buffs directly off the Auction House right before raid time. 

Here is how you turn your catches into gold:

```
+------------------------+--------------------------+-------------------------+
| Raw Catch              | Cooking Recipe           | Raider Buff Granted     |
+------------------------+--------------------------+-------------------------+
| Winter Squid           | Grilled Squid            | +10 Agility             |
| Raw Nightfin Snapper   | Nightfin Soup            | +8 MP5 (Mana Regen)     |
| Large Raw Mightfish    | Mightfish Steak          | +10 Stamina             |
| Raw Spotted Yellowtail | Spotted Yellowtail       | +1930 Health (No buff)  |
+------------------------+--------------------------+-------------------------+
```

### The Recipe Shopping List
1.  **Recipe: Grilled Squid**
    *   *Vendor:* Bought from **Kelsey Yance** in Booty Bay (Stranglethorn Vale). 
    *   *Why it's BiS:* This is the absolute best food buff for Rogues, Hunters, Feral Druids, and DPS Warriors in the game. It is in massive demand year-round.
2.  **Recipe: Nightfin Soup**
    *   *Vendor:* Bought from **Gikkix** in Gadgetzan (Tanaris).
    *   *Why it's BiS:* Every single Holy Priest, Restoration Druid, Restoration Shaman, and Holy Paladin eats this on every raid boss. It is a staple of the raiding economy.
3.  **Recipe: Mightfish Steak**
    *   *Vendor:* Bought from **Sheendra Tallgrass** in Feathermoon Stronghold (right where you are fishing!).
    *   *Why it's BiS:* Tanks love the +10 Stamina boost.
4.  **Recipe: Spotted Yellowtail**
    *   *Vendor:* Bought from **Gikkix** in Gadgetzan.

*Note: Stonescale Eels cannot be cooked. Sell these raw to Alchemists, or mail them to an Alchemy alt to craft **Stonescale Oil**, which is used in Flask of the Titans and Greater Stoneshield Potions.*

## Chapter IV: The Clock & The Calendar (When to Fish)

WoW Classic utilizes a real-time day/night cycle and a seasonal calendar. To maximize your gold/hour, you must fish at the correct times.

```
   00:00 (Midnight) ----------------------------------> 06:00 (AM)
                     PEAK STONESCALE EEL & NIGHTFIN
   
   06:00 (AM) ----------------------------------------> 18:00 (6 PM)
                     PEAK SUNSCALE SALMON & YELLOWTAIL
                     
   18:00 (6 PM) --------------------------------------> 24:00 (Midnight)
                     STONESCALE EEL & NIGHTFIN START APPEARING
```

### The Seasonal Factor: The Winter Squid Window
*   **Winter Season (September 23 – March 19):** Winter Squid can be caught. During this time, your gold per hour at **Feathermoon Stronghold** sky-rockets.
*   **Summer Season (March 20 – September 22):** Winter Squid is replaced by Raw Summer Bass (which cooks into *Hot Smoked Bass*, a low-value Spirit food). During summer, you should pivot to **Moonglade** for Nightfin Snapper.

Hold your stock in your bank and flood the Auction House on **Tuesday afternoon and Wednesday evening** (standard raid reset times). When Summer hits, the supply of Winter Squid drops to zero, but the demand from physical DPS classes remains identical. You can easily double your gold per hour by selling your winter backlog during the dead of summer.
