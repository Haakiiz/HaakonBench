# Level 60 Alliance Warrior “Do-Not-Move” Fishing Gold Guide  
**WoW Classic / Era-style servers — 300 Fishing, 300 Cooking**

Your constraints change the normal fishing meta a lot:

- **No moving** means we mostly ignore floating pools. Pools are often best gold/hour, but they require roaming.
- **Not being killed** means Alliance towns, guarded docks, neutral/sanctuary-style zones, or cleared instances are king.
- **300 Fishing + 300 Cooking** means you can turn certain fish into raid consumables or vanity food for a higher margin.
- **Level 60 Warrior** means you can safely clear low-level instances like Wailing Caverns and then fish in peace.

The best overall strategy is to have **three “anchor spots”** depending on time of day and market prices:

1. **Wailing Caverns instance** — safest true no-PvP option, Deviate Fish/Savory Deviate Delight.
2. **Moonglade, Lake Elune’ara** — extremely safe Nightfin/Sunscale spot.
3. **Feathermoon Stronghold docks, Feralas** — Alliance-guarded ocean fishing for Stonescale Eel, Squid/Bass, Mightfish.

# Spot 1: Wailing Caverns — The True “I Cannot Be Ganked” Farm

## Why This Is Excellent

This is the safest stationary gold farm in the entire guide.

Once you are inside the instance and have cleared a fishing area, you are protected from enemy players. No Horde rogue can appear behind you. No world PvP. No patrols if you clear correctly.

The target fish:

- **Deviate Fish**
- Cook into **Savory Deviate Delight**

Savory Deviate Delight is not a power consumable; it is a vanity/cosmetic food. That makes it weirdly resilient. People buy it for fun, PvP, duels, roleplay, and general nonsense.

## Location

- **Wailing Caverns**, The Barrens.
- As Alliance, the travel is the risky part, especially on a PvP server.
- Once inside, you are safe.

## Setup

1. Enter Wailing Caverns.
2. Clear to a comfortable water spot.
3. Park yourself near the water.
4. Fish indefinitely.
5. Cook Deviate Fish into **Savory Deviate Delight** if the cooked version sells for more than the raw fish.

## What to Sell

Check both prices:

- **Raw Deviate Fish**
- **Savory Deviate Delight**

Usually, Savory Deviate Delight sells better, but not always. If the AH is flooded, raw fish may be better.

## Estimated Gold/Hour

Typical stationary catch rate: roughly **50–80 Deviate Fish/hour**.

Example prices:

| Deviate / SDD Price | Approx. Gold/Hour |
|---:|---:|
| 40s each | 20–32g/hr |
| 75s each | 37–60g/hr |
| 1g each | 50–80g/hr |
| 1.25g+ each | 60–100g/hr |

## My Verdict

If your server has strong Savory Deviate Delight prices, this is your best “never die, never move” option.

**Best for:** maximum safety, all-day fishing, PvP servers, lazy gold.

# Spot 2: Moonglade — Lake Elune’ara Nightfin Farm

## Why This Is Great

Moonglade is one of the safest high-level freshwater fishing locations. Very low danger, peaceful environment, and you can fish from the shore without pulling mobs.

The valuable fish:

- **Raw Nightfin Snapper**
- Cook into **Nightfin Soup**
- Also catches **Raw Sunscale Salmon** during the day

Nightfin Soup is a healer/caster mana regeneration food. It sells best before raid nights.

## Location

Fish from the shore of **Lake Elune’ara** near Nighthaven.

Good parking areas:

- Around the Nighthaven shore.
- Near the docks/settlement.
- Any quiet piece of shoreline with no mobs nearby.

**18:00–06:00 server time**, with the late night / early morning window often being strongest.

Target:

- **Night:** Raw Nightfin Snapper → Nightfin Soup

> Cooked sale price - reagent cost - AH cut  
> versus  
> raw fish sale price

Sometimes raw sells better because lazy cooks or alchemists buy in bulk. Do not automatically cook everything.

# Example Gold/Hour Calculation

Use this formula:

> Gold/hour = fish per hour × sale price per fish or cooked item  
> minus reagent cost  
> minus AH cut

Example:

You fish **60 Deviate Fish/hour**.

Savory Deviate Delight sells for **90s each**.

Reagent cost is tiny.

Approximate:

> 60 × 0.9g = 54g/hr

After AH cuts and spices:

> roughly **50g/hr**

Another example:

You fish **40 Nightfin/hour**.

Nightfin Soup sells for **60s each**.

> 40 × 0.6g = 24g/hr

After water/AH cut:

> roughly **22g/hr**
