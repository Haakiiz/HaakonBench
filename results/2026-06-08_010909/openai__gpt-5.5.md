Your best strategy is not one location; it’s a **clock-based rotation**. The most valuable Classic fish are tied to **time of day**, **season**, or **raid-consumable demand**. Since you want to fish for long stretches without moving and without dying, we’ll avoid pool-chasing and focus on **safe open-water spots**.

Assumptions for gold/hour:

- You have **300 Fishing / 300 Cooking**.
- You are fishing open water from one spot, not chasing pools.
- Estimates assume ~130–160 successful catches/hour.
- Gold/hour varies heavily by server economy.
- Travel time is excluded.
- Cooking time is roughly included for cooked-food strategies, but AH cuts are not always included.
- On PvP servers, “contested” is never 100% safe, only low-risk.

# Chapter 1: The Best Overall Spot — Feathermoon Stronghold

## Location

**Feathermoon Stronghold, Feralas**  
Stand on the docks or shoreline of Feathermoon and cast into the open ocean.

This is probably your best “serious gold but still safe-ish” Alliance-friendly spot.

### Why it is good

- Alliance town with guards.
- Low Horde traffic compared to Tanaris, STV, or Azshara.
- No hostile mobs on the dock.
- You can fish from one position for hours.
- High-value saltwater catches.
- Excellent for both **Stonescale Eel** and **Winter Squid**.

### What to fish

#### Night: Stonescale Eel

Best window:

- **00:00–06:00 server time**
- Usable window starts around evening, but midnight to dawn is best.

Use:

- Sell raw **Stonescale Eel**.
- Or convert to **Stonescale Oil** if you have an alchemist and the oil sells better.

Demand comes from:

- Greater Stoneshield Potions
- Defensive consumables
- Alchemy users
- Raid tanks and progression guilds

Estimated catch value:

- Common: **12–25g/hour**
- Good market: **25–35g/hour**
- Excellent market: **40g+/hour**

Do not cook Stonescale Eels. They are an alchemy fish.

---

#### Winter daytime: Raw Winter Squid

Best window:

- During Winter Squid season: approximately **September 23 to March 19**
- Best during daytime server hours.

Cook into:

> **Grilled Squid**  
> Raw Winter Squid + Soothing Spices

Grilled Squid gives **+10 Agility**, making it desirable for:

- Rogues
- Hunters
- Feral druids
- Fury warriors, depending on server/meta
- Twinks and parse-minded players

Estimated value:

- Average market: **14–25g/hour**
- Strong raid market: **25–40g/hour**

Important tip:  
When Winter Squid season is ending, stockpile. During the off-season, Grilled Squid often rises in price because nobody can fish new squid.

---

#### Bycatch

At Feathermoon you may also catch useful high-level saltwater fish such as:

- Large Raw Mightfish → **Mightfish Steak**, +10 Stamina
- Darkclaw Lobster → **Lobster Stew**, high-level food
- Raw Spotted Yellowtail → cooked/vendor/leveling food
- Raw Whitescale Salmon or similar high-level food fish depending on water table

These are not usually the main moneymakers, but they add background value.

# Chapter 3: The Creative PvP-Proof Option — Wailing Caverns Instance

This is one of the most underrated “I do not want to be killed” fishing setups.

## Location

**Wailing Caverns, Northern Barrens**

Yes, it is Horde territory. But once you are inside the instance, enemy players cannot touch you.

As a level 60 warrior, you can clear your way to fishable water easily. Once the area is clear, stand still and fish.

### What to fish

**Deviate Fish**

Use one of two selling strategies:

### Option A: Sell raw Deviate Fish

Good if the recipe is rare or cooked margins are poor.

### Option B: Cook into Savory Deviate Delight

Requires:

> **Recipe: Savory Deviate Delight**

This is a rare Barrens drop, often expensive. If you do not have it, check whether buying it is worth it.

Cook:

> Deviate Fish + Mild Spices → Savory Deviate Delight

Why it sells:

- Pirate/ninja transformations
- Roleplay
- PvP memes
- Twinks
- Collectors
- General novelty demand

Estimated value:

- Weak cosmetic market: **8–12g/hour**
- Normal market: **12–22g/hour**
- Strong market: **22–30g/hour**
- Weird servers: can spike higher

### Safety note

The travel to Wailing Caverns can be dangerous on a PvP server because the Barrens is hostile territory for Alliance. But once inside, it is one of the safest long-session fishing options in the game.

Trash respawns eventually, but it is low-level and cannot realistically kill a level 60 warrior unless you are completely inattentive.

# Chapter 10: Warrior-Specific Safety Setup

You are a warrior, which means:

- No stealth.
- No vanish.
- No blink.
- Fishing pole equipped means no real weapon.
- If someone opens on you in a contested zone, you are vulnerable for a moment.

Fix that with preparation.

## Keep a weapon swap macro

Example:

```text
/equip Your One-Hander
/equip Your Shield
/cast Defensive Stance
```

Or, if you prefer your two-hander:

```text
/equip Your Two-Hander
/cast Berserker Stance
```

Keep at least one bag slot free so weapon swapping does not fail.

## Bring

- 20–40 Aquadynamic Fish Attractors or Bright Baubles for long sessions
- Simple Wood
- Flint and Tinder
- Soothing Spices
- Mild Spices
- Refreshing Spring Water
- Empty bags
- Hearthstone set somewhere convenient
- Shield and real weapon

## Camera setup

When fishing in contested zones:

- Zoom camera out.
- Face your character toward the water, but angle camera to watch behind you.
- Turn on game sound.
- Fish near guards if possible.
- Do not stand on beaches outside guard range.
