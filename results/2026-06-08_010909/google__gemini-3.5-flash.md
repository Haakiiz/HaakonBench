# The Patient Gladiator’s Guide to Wealth
### A Level 60 Alliance Warrior’s Guide to Safe, High-Yield Fishing & Cooking in WoW Classic (Era)

As a level 60 Warrior, your life is usually defined by rage, blood, high repair bills, and fighting for every single mob tag in Silithus or the Eastern Plaguelands. 

But there is another way. A way where your plate armor stays clean, your repair bill remains at zero, and the gold flows directly into your bags while you watch a movie or read on your second monitor. 

This guide is tailored specifically for a **Level 60 Human Warrior** with **300 Fishing** and **300 Cooking**. We will bypass the dangerous, highly contested PvP zones and focus on **absolute safety, Alliance-guarded bastions, and sanctuary zones** where you can fish for hours without moving a single yard, converting raw catches into raid-essential consumables for maximum Gold per Hour (G/H).

## Chapter 1: The Warrior’s Tackle Box (Preparation)

Before casting your first line, you must optimize your gear. In high-level zones, fish will escape if your effective fishing skill is too low. To ensure **zero missed catches**, you want to push your fishing skill as close to (or beyond) the zone caps.

### 1. The Weapon of Choice
*   **Nat Pagle’s Extreme Angler FC-5000 (+25 Fishing):** As an Alliance player, this is a must-have. It is a reward from the quest *"Snapjaws, Mon!"* in the Hinterlands. It is completely safe to acquire at level 60.
*   *Alternative:* **Big Iron Fishing Pole (+20 Fishing):** Found in Shellfish Cages in Desolace.

### 2. The Attire
*   **Lucky Fishing Hat (+5 Fishing):** Won from the Stranglethorn Vale Fishing Extravaganza. (Optional, but highly recommended).
*   **Nat Pagle's Extreme Anglin' Boots (+5 Fishing):** Another STV Derby prize.
*   **High-Test Eternium Line (+5 Fishing):** Attached to your fishing pole (applied by Engineers or bought on the AH).

### 3. The Consumables
To fish in high-level waters without "Your fish got away" messages, you must use lures:
*   **Bright Baubles (+75 Fishing / 10 mins):** Sold by any fishing vendor. Cheap and reliable.
*   **Aquadynamic Fish Lens (+100 Fishing / 10 mins):** Best in slot. Buy these in bulk from the Auction House or have an Engineer friend craft them.

### Location I: The Moonglade Sanctuary (The Infinite MP5 Farm)
*   **Safety Rating:** 10/10 (Absolute Sanctuary - PvP is physically impossible here)
*   **Target Fish:** Nightfin Snapper (Nighttime) / Sunscale Salmon (Daytime)
*   **Required Fishing Skill:** 300 (No lures required, but recommended for speed)

#### The Strategy:
Moonglade is a neutral sanctuary. If a Horde player attacks you here, the level 60 Elite Moonglade Wardens will instantly teleport them to their death. You can stand on the shores of Lake Elune'ara completely naked, and you will never die.

*   **When to fish:** 
    *   **6:00 PM to 6:00 AM (Server Time):** This is your prime window. The catch rate for **Nightfin Snapper** rises to ~30-40%. 
    *   **6:00 AM to 6:00 PM (Server Time):** The lake transitions to **Sunscale Salmon** (~30-40% catch rate).
*   **The Cooking Conversion:** Convert your Nightfin Snappers into **Nightfin Soup** using 1x Nightfin and 1x Refreshing Spring Water (bought from the vendor right next to the lake in Nighthaven). 

#### Estimated Gold/Hour: 
*   **Daytime (Sunscale):** 25 - 35 Gold/Hour
*   **Nighttime (Nightfin):** **45 - 60 Gold/Hour** (Nightfin Soup is highly contested on raid reset nights).

### Location II: Theramore Isle Docks, Dustwallow Marsh (The Guarded Ocean)
*   **Safety Rating:** 9.5/10 (Alliance Territory, heavily guarded by Level 55+ Guards)
*   **Target Fish:** Stonescale Eel, Winter Squid, Oily Blackmouth
*   **Required Fishing Skill:** 350+ (Use a +75/100 Lure)

#### The Strategy:
Theramore is an Alliance stronghold. You will fish directly off the wooden docks near the Southern Sea side of the city. Horde players cannot approach you without pulling 5-10 elite guards. 

*   **When to fish:** 
    *   **6:00 PM to 6:00 AM:** This is **Stonescale Eel** peak time. Stonescale Eels make up ~30% of your catches during these hours.
    *   **12:00 PM to 6:00 PM:** This is **Winter Squid** peak time (Note: Winter Squid can *only* be caught between September 23 and March 19).
*   **The Cooking/Economy Conversion:** 
    *   Cook the Winter Squid into **Grilled Squid** (+10 Agility). This is the BiS food buff for physical DPS.
    *   Stonescale Eels cannot be cooked. However, you can mail them to an Alchemist alt or friend to craft **Stonescale Oil**, which sells for significantly more than the raw eel.

#### Estimated Gold/Hour:
*   **During Winter (Squid + Eel mix):** **55 - 75 Gold/Hour**
*   **During Summer (Eel + Raw Mightfish mix):** 40 - 55 Gold/Hour

#### Estimated Gold/Hour:
*   **Immediate Sale (Winter):** 50 - 65 Gold/Hour

## Chapter 4: The Warrior’s Lazy Fishing Setup (Semi-AFK)

Since you want to fish for extended periods without moving, you need to set up your interface so you can watch a movie, read, or work on another screen.

### 1. The One-Handed Fishing Macro
You don't want to keep reaching for your keyboard. Bind this macro to a mouse button (like Mouse 4 or Mouse 5):

```macro
/cast [noequipped:Fishing Poles] Nat Pagle's Extreme Angler FC-5000
/cast Fishing
```
*This macro automatically equips your fishing pole and casts the line with a single click.*

### 2. Audio Setup for "Blind" Fishing
You don't need to watch the screen to catch fish. You only need to hear the **"splash"** sound.
1. Go to **System > Sound**.
2. Uncheck **Music** and **Ambience**.
3. Turn **Sound Effects** to maximum.
4. Check **Sound in Background** (This allows you to hear the splash even if WoW is minimized or on your second monitor).
5. When you hear the *SPLASH*, simply right-click the bobber.

### 3. Essential Addons
*   **Fishing Buddy:** Tracks your catches per zone, automatically applies lures with a double-right-click, and displays your current fishing skill.
*   **Faster Looting:** Instantly loots the fish without showing the loot window transition, saving you 1-2 seconds per cast.

## Chapter 5: The Auction House Playbook (Selling for Max Value)

As a Warrior, you understand the art of war. The Auction House is just another battlefield. To get the maximum gold for your cooked fish, follow these rules:

1.  **The Tuesday/Wednesday Raid Reset Peak:** 
    Do not post your fish on Friday or Saturday. The best time to list **Nightfin Soup** and **Grilled Squid** is **Tuesday afternoon (around 4:00 PM server time)** right before guilds start their weekly raid runs. This is when prices skyrocket due to last-minute buying.
2.  **The Stack Size Secret:** 
    Raiders want convenience. Sell your cooked food in **stacks of 10 or 20**. Do not sell them in singles. Raiders will gladly pay a 10-15% premium markup just to buy a full stack of 20 rather than clicking 20 individual auctions.
3.  **Underpricing Bait:** 
    If you see someone listing single fish for very cheap, buy them out, cook them, repackage them into stacks of 20, and resell them at your premium price.
