**The Peaceful Vanguard: A Level 60 Human Warrior’s Guide to Monopolizing the Culinary Market**

As a Level 60 Human Warrior, you spend your days tanking dragons, charging into the fray, and paying exorbitant repair bills. You deserve a break. But a warrior’s break shouldn’t mean a halt in income. 

Since you require absolute safety, zero movement, and maximum utilization of your 300 Fishing / 300 Cooking, we are going to turn you into a stationary gold-printing machine. We will focus on high-end raid consumables and PvP buffs, which are the lifeblood of the WoW Classic Era economy.

Here is your comprehensive, master-class strategy to dominate the market without ever drawing your sword.

### Chapter 1: The Warrior’s Tackle Box (Preparation)
Before you plant your boots by the water, you need the right setup. Stationary fishing is about efficiency.
*   **The Gear:** Arcanite Fishing Pole (if you won the STV tournament) or Big Iron Fishing Pole. Keep +Fishing lures (Aquadynamic Fish Attractors) on hand. Even at 300, high-level zones have a "get away" rate unless you are sitting at 400+ effective fishing skill.
*   **The UI:** Download the addon **FishingBuddy**. It allows you to double-click the water to cast, tracks your catch rates, and auto-applies lures. 
*   **The Bags:** Clear your inventory. You want at least 40 free slots. 
*   **The Spices:** Buy stacks of Soothing Spices and Hot Spices from a vendor *before* you leave. You will cook on location.

### Chapter 2: The Golden Locations

*Disclaimer: Gold Per Hour (GPH) varies depending on your server's economy (Era, Hardcore, or Progression). The estimates below are based on mature Classic Era economies where raid consumables are in high demand.*

#### Strategy A: The Feathermoon Monopoly (Feralas)
**The Vibe:** Coastal, relaxing, Alliance-controlled.
**The Location:** Feathermoon Stronghold, Feralas. Walk to the very back of the island, behind the Inn or the Moonwell. You are surrounded by level 60 elite Alliance guards. Horde rogues will not survive attempting to gank you here.
**Safety Rating:** 10/10.

*   **Time:** **Daytime (6:00 AM - 6:00 PM) - *WINTER ONLY***
    *   **Target:** Winter Squid.
    *   **The Cooking Play:** Cook into **Grilled Squid** (+10 Agility). This is the Best-in-Slot food for Rogues and Hunters. 
    *   **Market Note:** Winter Squid can only be caught between the Fall and Spring Equinoxes (Sept 23 - March 20). If it is Summer, *do not fish here during the day.*
    *   **Estimated GPH:** 50g - 80g/hr.

*   **Time:** **Nighttime (12:00 AM - 6:00 AM)**
    *   **Target:** Stonescale Eel.
    *   **The Cooking Play:** DO NOT COOK. Send raw to your bank alt. Alchemists need these for Stonescale Oil, which makes **Flask of the Titans**. As a Warrior, you know how vital these are.
    *   **Estimated GPH:** 60g - 100g/hr (Stonescale Eels are liquid gold on raid nights).

#### Strategy B: The Instanced Anomaly (Wailing Caverns)
**The Vibe:** Subterranean, completely hidden, perfectly safe.
**The Location:** Wailing Caverns instance, The Barrens. Run past the horde guards (you are level 60, they will miss or you can ignore them), enter the actual instance portal. Walk 10 yards to the first pool of water. Clear the two level 18 raptors. Sit down.
**Safety Rating:** 11/10. You are in an instance. It is impossible to be ganked. You can sit here for 24 hours.

*   **Time:** 24/7 (Time of day does not matter).
    *   **Target:** Deviate Fish.
    *   **The Cooking Play:** Cook into **Savory Deviate Delight**. (You *must* farm or buy the Recipe: Savory Deviate Delight first).
    *   **Market Note:** Players are addicted to turning into Ninjas and Pirates. This is a cosmetic item, but it sells faster than raid food because PvPers and levelers buy them constantly. 
    *   **Estimated GPH:** 40g - 60g/hr.

**The Vibe:** Nostalgic, rainy, zero-stress.
**The Location:** Menethil Harbor docks, Wetlands. Sit at the end of the northernmost dock. It is a level 20 Alliance zone. Horde rarely come here, and if they do, they are low-level travelers waiting for a boat.
**Safety Rating:** 9.5/10.

    *   **Target:** Oily Blackmouth & Firefin Snapper.

Because it's a lower-level zone, the catch rate is incredibly fast, and you will never miss a catch. Volume is the name of the game here.
    *   **Estimated GPH:** 30g - 45g/hr. (Lower gold per fish, but massive volume).

#### Strategy D: The Hidden Caster's Oasis (Azshara)
**The Vibe:** Isolated, beautiful, slightly risky but highly rewarding.
**The Location:** The Bay of Storms, Azshara. Ride to the far eastern cliffs. Use a Levitate potion (or just carefully drop down) to a small, isolated rock structure off the coast. Most players don't know these rocks exist, and you cannot be seen from the mainland.
**Safety Rating:** 8/10 (Contested zone, but functionally invisible).

*   **Time:** **Nighttime (6:00 PM - 6:00 AM)**
    *   **Target:** Stonescale Eel & Glossy Mightfish.
    *   **The Cooking Play:** Cook Mightfish into **Mightfish Steak** (+10 Stamina). Sell Stonescale Eels raw.
    *   **Estimated GPH:** 70g - 110g/hr. (Azshara has the highest drop rate for Stonescale Eels in the game, but requires +400 fishing skill with lures to not let them get away).

### Chapter 3: The Cooking Multiplier (Maximizing Profits)

Having 300 Cooking is what separates the peasants from the barons. Here is how you utilize it stationary:

1.  **The Campfire:** Buy a stack of Simple Wood and Flint and Tinder. Whenever your bags fill up with raw fish, pop a Basic Campfire right where you are sitting.
2.  **Batch Cooking:** Cook your Winter Squid and Deviate Fish on the spot. This frees up bag space, allowing you to stay stationary for 4-5 hours at a time without needing to visit a bank.
3.  **Trash Fish:** You will catch Raw Spotted Yellowtail. Cook them into Spotted Yellowtail and vendor them, or mail them to an alt to vendor. Do not waste AH deposit fees on them.

### Chapter 4: The Auction House Tactics

You are spending hours pulling wealth from the sea; don't fumble it at the market.

*   **The Tuesday Spike:** Post your Grilled Squid and Stonescale Eels on **Tuesday afternoons**. This is when raid lockouts reset. Guilds are buying consumables in bulk. You can mark up your prices by 15-20% and they *will* sell.
*   **The Weekend Warrior:** Post your Savory Deviate Delights and Free Action Potion materials on **Friday evenings**. This is when the PvP bracket grinders are stocking up for weekend Battlegrounds.
*   **Stack Sizes:** 
    *   Sell Grilled Squid in stacks of 5 or 10 (perfect for one raid night).
    *   Sell Savory Deviate Delights in stacks of 10 or 20.
    *   Sell Stonescale Eels in stacks of 20.
