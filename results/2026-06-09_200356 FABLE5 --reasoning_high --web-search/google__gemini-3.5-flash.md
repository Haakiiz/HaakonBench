## Chapter 1: The Zen of the Warrior-Angler

You have spent months taking crushing blows from Raid Bosses, grinding out your Lionheart Helm, and bleeding gold on astronomical repair bills. You are a Level 60 Human Warrior—the master of rage, steel, and battlefield dominance. 

But constantly grinding elite mobs or running dungeons for gold is exhausting. Sometimes, you just want to sit back, let your rage bar deplete, and watch your gold bar grow. 

Fishing is the ultimate gold-making equalizer for Warriors. It has **no gear requirements, no repair bills, and no complex rotations**. By pairing your **300 Fishing** with your **300 Cooking**, you can transform raw, cheap catches into high-end, raid-essential consumables. 

This guide is designed specifically for **stationary, long-session farming**. You will not have to run around chasing pools. You will sit in one spot, completely safe from mobs and players, while rakers of gold flow directly into your bags.

## Chapter 2: The Armory (Gearing Up to 425+ Skill)

High-level zones like Azshara and Feralas require high fishing skill to prevent the dreaded *"Your fish got away"* message. While your base skill is 300, you want to push your effective skill as high as possible.

### The Pole
*   **Nat Pagle's Extreme Angler FC-5000 (+25 Fishing):** The premier choice. Obtained from the Alliance quest *"Snapjaws, Mon!"* in the Hinterlands. It’s easy, free, and highly effective.
*   **Big Iron Fishing Pole (+20 Fishing):** A solid alternative, found inside Shellfish Traps along the coast of Desolace.

### The Lures
*   **Aquadynamic Fish Lens (+100 Fishing for 10 mins):** Essential for Azshara. Bought from limited vendors or crafted by Engineers. Keep a stack of these for high-yield sessions.
*   **Bright Baubles (+75 Fishing for 10 mins):** Your everyday lure. Cheap, sold by any basic fishing vendor, and perfectly sufficient for Moonglade and Feralas.

### Enchants & Extras
*   **Enchant Gloves - Fishing (+2 Fishing):** A cheap, permanent enchant.
*   **Lucky Fishing Hat (+5 Fishing) / Nat Pagle's Extreme Anglin' Boots (+5 Fishing):** Rewards from the Stranglethorn Fishing Extravaganza. If you have them, wear them!

## Chapter 3: The Culinary Codex (Recipes & Synergy)

Raw fish sells decently, but **cooked fish is where the true wealth lies**. Raiders are lazy; they want to buy raid-ready buff food directly from the Auction House. Before you cast a single line, visit **Gikkix in Steamwheedle Port, Tanaris** to purchase these three essential recipes:

1.  **Recipe: Grilled Squid** (+10 Agility for 10 mins)
    *   *Target Audience:* Rogues, Hunters, Feral Druids, and DPS Warriors.
2.  **Recipe: Nightfin Soup** (+8 Mana per 5 seconds)
    *   *Target Audience:* Holy Paladins, Priests, Resto Druids, Mages, and Warlocks.
3.  **Recipe: Mightfish Steak** (+10 Stamina for 15 mins)
    *   *Target Audience:* Tanks.

*Note on Stonescale Eels:* Do not cook these (they cannot be cooked). Keep them raw. Alchemists buy them in bulk to turn into Stonescale Oil, which is a core ingredient for Flasks (Titans, Supreme Power).

### Spot A: Feathermoon Stronghold Docks (Feralas)
*   **The Setup:** Fly to Feathermoon Stronghold on Sardor Isle (Feralas). Walk onto the wooden docks right near the flight master. 
*   **Safety:** **10/10.** This is an Alliance military fortress. Level 55+ Elite Sentinel guards patrol the docks. If a stray Horde player wanders by, the guards will instantly shred them. There are absolutely no hostile mobs.
*   **Target Catch:** Stonescale Eel, Winter Squid (Seasonal), Large Raw Mightfish.
*   **The Vibe:** Completely brainless. You are flagged for PvP only if you are on a PvP server, but even then, no sane Horde player will attack you under the bows of twenty Elite Sentinels.

### Spot B: Lake Elune'ara (Moonglade)
*   **The Setup:** Stand on the wooden platforms or sandy shores of Lake Elune'ara, just south of Nighthaven.
*   **Safety:** **9.5/10.** Moonglade is a neutral druidic sanctuary. On PvP servers, attacking another player in Moonglade triggers a swarm of furious Level 60 Elite Keepers. Furthermore, there is an unspoken community "Druid Code" of absolute peace in this zone. No hostile mobs walk near the lake.
*   **Target Catch:** Raw Nightfin Snapper, Raw Redgill.
*   **The Vibe:** Peaceful, mystical, and quiet. You can fish alongside Horde players in perfect harmony.

### Spot C: The Sunken Gazebo (Azshara)
*   **The Setup:** Swim out to the subzone of *Haetara's Clutch* in the Bay of Storms (around coordinates 53, 60). You will find a half-sunken, circular Elven gazebo in the water. Climb on top of the dome and fish directly into the center of the structure.
*   **Safety:** **7.5/10.** While Azshara is a contested zone populated by Level 55+ Elite Giants, **no land mobs can path onto this dome**. It is also highly hidden; passing players on the cliffs above will almost never spot you.
*   **Target Catch:** High-yield Stonescale Eel, Winter Squid (Seasonal), Large Raw Mightfish.
*   **Requirements:** This is a maximum-difficulty water zone. You *must* have 425+ effective fishing skill to prevent fish from escaping. Use Nat Pagle's Pole + Aquadynamic Lures (+100).

## Chapter 5: The Chronology (The 6-Hour Rule & Seasons)

WoW Classic fishing relies heavily on the server’s internal clock and seasonal cycles. To maximize your gold per hour, you must align your real-world fishing sessions with these mechanics.

### The 6-Hour Rule
The catch rates of high-value fish fluctuate across four distinct 6-hour windows:

*   **12:00 AM (Midnight) to 6:00 AM — *The Golden Nightfin Window*:** 
    *   **Best for Moonglade.** Nightfin Snapper catch rates peak at roughly **30% to 35%**. Sunscale Salmon (the useless daytime placeholder) cannot be caught at all during this window. Stonescale Eel catch rates also peak during this time.
*   **12:00 PM (Noon) to 6:00 PM — *The Golden Squid Window*:** 
    *   **Best for Feralas & Azshara.** Winter Squid catch rates peak here. Nightfin Snappers cannot be caught.
*   **6:00 AM to 12:00 PM** & **6:00 PM to 12:00 AM:** 
    *   Transition periods. Average catch rates for all species.

### The Seasonal Cycle
*   **The Winter Season (September 22 – March 19):** **Winter Squid is active.** This is your peak gold-making season. Stockpile as much Grilled Squid as your bank alts can hold.
*   **The Summer Season (March 20 – September 21):** **Raw Summer Bass is active.** Summer Bass cooks into Hot Smoked Bass (+10 Spirit), which is far less valuable. During summer, pivot your primary focus to **Nightfin Snapper** (Moonglade) and **Stonescale Eels** (Feralas/Azshara).

### The Economy Playbook
1.  **Never Dump Your Stock:** Do not post 100 Nightfin Soups or Grilled Squids on the Auction House at once. This drives the price down. Post in **stacks of 5, 10, or 20** (standard raid consumable sizes).
2.  **The Tuesday/Wednesday Spike:** Raiders buy their consumables on **raid reset days** (typically Tuesdays and Wednesdays). Prices spike significantly during these evenings. Buy raw fish cheap on the weekend, cook them, and sell the finished products on raid nights.
3.  **The Summer Stockpile:** During the summer months, the supply of Winter Squid completely dries up. If you have bank space, stockpile cooked **Grilled Squid** during the winter and sell it at a massive premium during the summer to desperate, min-maxing physical DPS raiders.
