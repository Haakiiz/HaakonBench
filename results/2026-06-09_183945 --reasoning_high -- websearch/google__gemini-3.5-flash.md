# The Lazy Gladiator’s Guide to Angling
### A Level 60 Human Warrior's Blueprint for Safe, Stationary Gold in WoW Classic

As a level 60 Human Warrior, your life is expensive. Between plate repair bills, Flasks of the Titans, Mighty Rage Potions, and Greater Stoneshield Potions, your raiding or PvP habits are draining your bank. Worse, your class isn't built for brainless, high-efficiency solo mob grinding unless you want to constantly eat, band-aid, or risk dying to PvP gankers in contested zones. 

This guide is designed for the ultimate "low-effort, high-reward" lifestyle. With **Level 300 Fishing** and **Level 300 Cooking**, you can stand completely still in safe, Alliance-friendly, or highly hidden sanctuaries, watch a movie, and pull in massive amounts of gold.

## Chapter 1: The Warrior's Angling Arsenal

Before you cast your first line, you need the right gear. In high-level zones, having a low fishing skill leads to the dreaded *"Your fish got away"* message. Your goal is to reach **425+ effective Fishing Skill** to fish high-end waters with 100% catch rates.

```
Base Skill (300) + Pole (+20) + Lure (+100) + Enchant (+2) = 422 Effective Skill
```

### 1. The Pole: Big Iron Fishing Pole (+20 Fishing)
*   **Why:** Unlike the Horde, Alliance players do not get an easy quest-reward pole. The **Big Iron Fishing Pole** (+20) is your best pre-tournament option.
*   **How to get it:** Head to the southern coast of Desolace (around Shadowprey Village). Dive underwater and open **Shellfish Traps**. At level 60, you can easily ignore or instantly execute the level 30-something crabs that spawn when you open them. It has a ~1.5% drop rate; you’ll have it in 20 minutes.
*   *Note: If you are truly dedicated, win the Sunday Stranglethorn Fishing Extravaganza for the **Arcanite Fishing Pole** (+35).*

### 2. The Enchants & Attachments
*   **Enchant Gloves - Fishing (+2 Fishing):** Ask an enchanter to put this on a spare pair of gloves. 
*   **High-Test Fishing Line (+2 Fishing):** Applied permanently to your pole. Won from the STV Fishing Tournament or bought on the AH.

### 3. The Lures
*   **Aquadynamic Fish Attractor (+100 Fishing):** Lasts 10 minutes. Buy them in bulk from the Auction House or have an Engineer friend craft them.
*   **Bright Baubles (+75 Fishing):** Lasts 10 minutes. Sold by any standard fishing vendor for a few silver. Use these for lower-level safe zones like Moonglade.

### Spot 1: The Sanctuary of Elune (Lake Elune'ara, Moonglade)
*   **Type:** Inland Water
*   **Safety:** **10/10.** Moonglade is a neutral druid sanctuary. If a Horde player so much as looks at you funny, the level 60 Elite Keepers of the Grove will instantly vaporize them. 
*   **When to Fish:** **6:00 PM to 6:00 AM** (Nightfin hours).
*   **The Strategy:** Stand right on the wooden docks of Nighthaven. Cast into the lake.
*   **Why it's profitable:** You will catch **Raw Nightfin Snapper** (at a ~25-30% rate during peak hours) and **Raw Sunscale Salmon**.

### Spot 2: The Sentinel's Docks (Feathermoon Stronghold, Feralas)
*   **Type:** Coastal Water
*   **Safety:** **10/10.** This is an Alliance island fortress. Horde players cannot reach you without taking a boat ride straight into level 55+ Elite Alliance Sentinels. 
*   **When to Fish:** 
    *   **12:00 PM to 6:00 PM** (For Winter Squid during Autumn/Winter).
    *   **12:00 AM to 6:00 AM** (For Stonescale Eels).
*   **The Strategy:** Fly to Feathermoon Stronghold, walk down to the southern docks, and cast into the ocean.
*   **Why it's profitable:** This coastal table yields **Winter Squid**, **Stonescale Eels**, **Large Raw Mightfish**, and **Oily Blackmouth**.

### Spot 3: The Hermit’s Ridge (Scalebeard's Island, Azshara)
*   **Type:** Coastal Water (Bay of Storms table)
*   **Safety:** **8.5/10.** Azshara is a contested zone, but the easternmost islands are a complete ghost town. 
*   **When to Fish:** **12:00 AM to 6:00 AM** (For maximum Stonescale Eel catch rates).
*   **The Strategy:** Swim out to **Scalebeard's Island** (the island in the far east of Azshara with the level 50 Elite dragon, Scalebeard). Do not go into the cave where Scalebeard lives. Instead, stand on the southern cliffs of the island. 
*   **Why it's profitable:** The Bay of Storms has the **highest raw catch rate for Stonescale Eels in the entire game (~23%)**. Because you are so far out, no players will ever run past you. It is incredibly remote and peaceful.

### The Warrior-Alchemy Synergy (The Stonescale Eel Loop)
As a Warrior, you cannot cook **Stonescale Eels**. However, they are used by Alchemists to make **Stonescale Oil**, which is the core ingredient for **Greater Stoneshield Potions** (essential for tanking) and **Flask of the Titans**. 
*   **Option A (The Solo Route):** Sell the raw Stonescale Eels in stacks of 20 on the AH. They are always in high demand.
*   **Option B (The Guild Partnership):** Send your eels to a trusted guild Alchemist. Have them craft **Stonescale Oil** or **Greater Stoneshield Potions** for you. Sell the finished potions on raid reset nights (usually Tuesdays/Thursdays) for a massive premium, or keep them to fuel your own tanking needs!

## Chapter 5: The "Netflix & Cast" Configuration

To fish for hours without losing your mind, you need to set up your UI and game audio so you can pay attention to a second monitor or a movie.

### 1. The Audio Trick (Fish by Sound)
You don't need to watch the screen to fish. You just need to hear the splash.
1. Go to **System > Audio**.
2. Uncheck **Music**.
3. Turn **Sound Effects** up to 100%.
4. Check **Sound in Background** (This allows you to hear the splash even if WoW is minimized or on your second monitor).
5. When you hear the *SPLASH*, click the bobber.

### 2. The One-Handed Keybind
To make fishing incredibly lazy, map your keys so you can cast and reel in with one hand.
*   Install the addon **Fishing Buddy**. It allows you to **double-right-click** on the water to cast your line, eliminating the need to click a button on your action bar.
*   Alternatively, go to your Keybindings and bind **"Interact with Target"** or **"Interact on Mouseover"** to an easily reachable key (like `G` or `Mouse 4`). Hover your mouse cursor over the bobber after casting, and simply press your key when you hear the splash.

### 3. The Warrior's Anti-Boredom Macro
Keep this macro on your bar to swap between your massive two-handed weapon and your fishing pole instantly:

```default
/equip [noequipped:Fishing Poles] Big Iron Fishing Pole
/equip [equipped:Fishing Poles] Your Two-Handed Weapon Name
```
